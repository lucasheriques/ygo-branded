Burst Protocol - New Branded Support

Burst Protocol (BPRO) released February 2026 with game-changing Branded support. These cards solve the deck's historical problems — extenders, recovery, and interaction during the opponent's turn.

Fallen of the White Dragon

Level 4 | DARK | Dragon | ATK 1800 / DEF 0

Always treated as "Fallen of Albaz"

Effect 1 — Self-Summon (from hand)

Send 1 monster that mentions "Fallen of Albaz" from your Extra Deck to the GY → Special Summon this card. Lock: Only Level 8 Fusion and Synchro Monsters from Extra Deck for the rest of the turn.

Why this is broken: The community calls this a "Circular" — the cost of sending an ED monster to GY actually sets up your plays (send Albion to GY for its trigger). The opponent literally cannot negate the cost, so the summon goes through with built-in advantage.

Effect 2 — Ecclesia Recruiter (on Summon)

If Normal or Special Summoned: Special Summon 1 "Ecclesia" monster from hand, Deck, or GY.

Key Targets: Incredible Ecclesia, the Virtuous (searches on summon for any Swordsoul or Dogmatika card) or the new Ecclesia synchro material.

Ecclesia of the Black Dragon (Synchro)

Level 8 | LIGHT | Spellcaster | Synchro | ATK 2500 / DEF 2500

1 Tuner + 1+ non-Tuner monsters

Effect 1 — Quick-Effect Banish + Recruit

(Quick Effect): Banish this card until End Phase → Special Summon 1 "Fallen of Albaz" or Level 4 or lower monster that mentions it from Deck or GY.

Why this matters: This is interaction during the OPPONENT'S turn. Banishes itself to dodge targeting, then recruits a monster — can grab Fallen of White Dragon for another Ecclesia summon, or get Cartesia for a quick-effect fusion during their turn.

Effect 2 — GY Shuffle Removal

If in GY: Target 1 Level 8 Fusion Monster in your GY/banished and 1 card on the field → shuffle all 3 into the Deck.

Removal + Recycling: Non-destroying removal that also recycles your Mirrorjade or other boss. Extremely strong going second or in grind games.

Ecclesia and the Dark Dragon (Fusion)

This is the TCG name for the fusion counterpart — a Level 8 DARK Fusion that pairs with the Branded Fusion engine.

Role in the deck: Accessible off Branded Fusion using Albaz + Ecclesia materials. Provides another body for the Branded in Red follow-up and synergizes with the Synchro Ecclesia's GY effect (which targets Level 8 Fusions).

Playing Against Branded — Where to Hand Trap

If you're facing Branded at regionals, here's exactly where each hand trap matters most.

Ash Blossom & Joyous Spring

Priority Target: Branded Fusion — This is the single most important negate. Without Branded Fusion resolving, the standard line collapses entirely. Do NOT Ash Aluber's search unless you are confident they don't have Branded Fusion already (e.g., they had to search for it).

Secondary: Fallen of White Dragon's recruit — Ash the Ecclesia summon from Deck. The FotW player still keeps the body but loses the Synchro play.

Note: If Branded Lost is active, Ash cannot negate Fusion Summons (Lost prevents responses). So use Ash BEFORE Lost comes online.

Effect Veiler / Infinite Impermanence

Target: Lubellion the Searing Dragon — Negate Lubellion to stop Mirrorjade from coming out. However, be aware that Cartesia can dodge targeting by chaining her quick-effect fusion.

Also good on: Aluber (if no Comedy in hand to protect it) or Bystial Lubellion (to stop Branded Lost activation).

Dimension Shifter

Devastating if resolved. Branded relies heavily on GY — Albion's End Phase set, Branded in Red targets, Bystial Lubellion's self-summon all need GY. D-Shifter blanks almost everything except the initial Branded Fusion.

Ghost Ogre & Snow Rabbit

Target: Branded Lost (continuous spell) — pop it before they start fusion summoning to remove the protection. Also good against Branded in Red if you can chain to its activation.

Droll & Lock Bird

After any search — Aluber searching lets you chain Droll. This stops all further adds for the turn, preventing Tragedy/Mercourier from searching, Bystial Lubellion from activating Branded Lost, etc. Very strong but timing-dependent.

Playing AS Branded — Protecting Your Plays

Baiting Ash Blossom

Always lead with Aluber or other search effects before committing Branded Fusion. If they Ash Aluber, your Branded Fusion is safe. If they don't, you searched and still have BF. Win-win sequencing.

Comedy (Despia)

From hand: protects a Despia monster on field from targeted effects (Impermanence/Veiler on Aluber). From GY: tribute a Fusion Monster to dodge targeting, then Comedy summons itself. Double protection layer.

Branded Lost

Once active, your Fusion Summons cannot be negated AND the opponent cannot activate effects in response to your Fusion Summons. This blanks Ash, Veiler, Imperm on your fusion plays. Get this online ASAP via Bystial Lubellion.

Fallen of White Dragon (backup plan)

If your Branded Fusion gets negated, Fallen of White gives you a completely independent line. Having both starters means one always gets through. This redundancy is what makes post-BPRO Branded so resilient.

Mercourier (in-engine counter)

When banished by Branded Fusion, Mercourier searches a Branded spell/trap. But its other effect is key: you can banish it from hand/GY to negate a monster effect that targets a Fusion Monster you control. Free omni-negate for your boss monsters.

Floodgates That Destroy You

Dimensional Barrier — Calling Fusion completely shuts you down for the turn. Side in spell/trap removal games 2-3.
Non-Fusion Area — Continuous trap that prevents all Fusion Summons. Must be removed immediately.
Macro Cosmos / Dimension Shifter — Banishing all cards going to GY cripples your entire resource loop.
Dominus Impulse — Reportedly one of the hardest cards for Branded to deal with in the current meta.

March 2026 TCG Meta — Key Matchups

Based on recent WCQ Regionals and OTS events. Branded is competitive with ~65% win rate at recent events.

Sky Striker Favored

Sky Striker is spell-heavy and grindy — Branded's constant fusion pressure overwhelms their resource management. Mirrorjade's banish is non-targeting non-destruction removal, which Sky Striker struggles to answer.

Side in: Solemn Report (banishes their key spells like Engage), Anti-Spell Fragrance. Watch for: Widow Anchor stealing your monsters, Shark Cannon banishing your GY resources.

Fiendsmith Yummy Even

Fiendsmith builds boards with strong negation. Your grind game is better but their ceiling boards can be hard to break. Mirrorjade's End Phase nuke helps reset their board.

Key: Don't overcommit into their negates. Use Ecclesia of the Black Dragon's GY shuffle to remove their key pieces without triggering destruction effects.

Mitsurugi Even

One of the newer meta decks. Can build strong boards. Your gas and recursion keep you competitive in grind games.

Key: Learn their combo starters so you know where to hand trap. Focus on stopping their setup rather than trying to break established boards.

K9 Vanquish Soul Even

Vanquish Soul plays a strong control game with hand reveals. Their disruption is attribute-based. Being DARK-heavy helps since they often check for DARK.

Side in: Extra removal for their backrow. Watch for: Stake Your Soul targeting your fusions.

Branded Dracotail (Mirror-ish) Even

Dracotail is a Branded variant using Dragonmaid package. Very similar resource game. Comes down to who resolves Branded Fusion first and manages resources better.

Key: Mercourier's targeted negate is MVP in this matchup. Save it for their Mirrorjade's banish effect.

Radiant Typhoon Slightly Favored

Spell-based combo deck. Branded's ability to play through interruption and grind gives an edge. Mirrorjade pressures their setup.

Side in: Solemn Report, Anti-Spell. Watch for: Their OTK potential going second.

Maliss Slightly Unfavored

Maliss builds interaction-heavy boards. @Ignister Maliss variants have shown strong results at YCS level (Sydney). Their recursion can match yours.

Key: Prioritize hand traps for their starters. Going first is important in this matchup.

Side Deck Considerations

Going Second: Evenly Matched, Lightning Storm, Dark Ruler No More, Kaijus. Branded naturally plays well going second due to Branded Fusion not needing board presence.
Anti-Spell/Trap Matchups: Solemn Report (excellent vs Sky Striker/Radiant Typhoon), Cosmic Cyclone, Twin Twisters.
Hand Traps: The deck runs fewer hand traps than most (engine-heavy). Common choices: Ash Blossom x2-3, Effect Veiler x1-2, or Nibiru in the side. Some players prefer Crossout Designator to protect Branded Fusion.

2-Week Regional Prep Plan

Target: Regionals ~March 28. Structured practice plan to get tournament-ready.

Days 1-2 (Mar 14-15) — Learn the Engine

Goldfish your combos solo (no opponent). Practice the 3 main lines above until you can do them without thinking. Use Dueling Nexus or EDOPro for free online practice. Goal: Execute Branded Fusion line, Fallen of White line, and combined line from memory.

Days 3-4 (Mar 16-17) — Hand Trap Navigation

Practice with a friend who hand traps you every game. Learn to play through 1 interruption. Key scenarios: Ash on Branded Fusion (pivot to FotW), Ash on FotW recruit (grind with Albion set), Imperm on Lubellion (use backup materials).

Days 5-7 (Mar 18-20) — Learn the Meta

Watch replays of top meta decks (Sky Striker, Fiendsmith, Mitsurugi, Maliss, K9 Vanquish Soul). Focus on identifying their starters and choke points. For each deck, know: what to Ash, what to Imperm, and what their end board looks like.

Days 8-10 (Mar 21-23) — Matchup Practice

Play 5+ games against each top meta deck. Focus on siding correctly. Track win rate. Adjust your side deck based on what you lose to most.

Days 11-12 (Mar 25-26) — Tournament Simulation

Play a full "tournament" — best of 3 matches, use your side deck, time yourself (40 min rounds). Practice your side deck plan for each matchup. Get comfortable with the pressure of timed rounds.

Day 13 (Mar 27) — Final Review

Review your decklist one final time. Sleeve your deck. Double-check you have: dice, calculator, pen/paper, extra sleeves, and your deck registration sheet filled out. Get good sleep.

Day 14 (Mar 28) — REGIONALS

Arrive early. Stay hydrated. Trust your practice. Remember: Branded's grind game is your advantage — don't panic if you lose game 1. Side deck wins events. Good luck!
