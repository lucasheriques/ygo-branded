Plain-English effects, the "why" behind every card, and what your opponents' cards do

The Big Picture — How Branded Actually Works

The Core Loop in One Sentence

You use Branded Fusion to dump monsters from your Deck to the GY as Fusion Materials, and those monsters generate free advantage when they hit the GY or get banished — so the "cost" of fusion summoning is actually pure profit.

Why You Get So Many Pluses

In most decks, Fusion Summoning is expensive — you lose 2+ cards to make 1 monster. Branded flips this on its head:

Step 1 — Branded Fusion sends from DECK (not hand)

You don't lose any cards from your hand. You send Fallen of Albaz + another monster straight from the Deck. That's already a +1 (you spent 1 spell, got 1 fusion monster, and didn't lose hand cards).

Step 2 — The monsters you send WANT to be in the GY

Albion in the GY → sets a free Branded spell/trap from your Deck during the End Phase. That's another +1.
Tragedy banished as material → searches any Despia monster from Deck. Another +1.
Mercourier banished → searches a Branded spell/trap. Another +1.
So your single Branded Fusion generates 2-4 cards of advantage.

Step 3 — Your Fusion Monsters are also stepping stones

Albion (first fusion) → uses its own effect to make Lubellion (second fusion) → Lubellion makes Mirrorjade (boss). Each fusion going to GY triggers more effects. One card (Branded Fusion) cascades into a full board.

Step 4 — You do it AGAIN on the opponent's turn

Branded in Red (set by Albion) lets you Fusion Summon during the opponent's turn using monsters in your GY. So your GY is a second hand. You build a board on your turn AND extend during theirs.

The Resource Flow (Visual)

Albaz gets shuffled back by Lubellion. Albion keeps setting free cards. You never run out.

Meta Decks Explained — What Are They Doing?

You'll face these at regionals. For each deck: what it does, its key cards, and where to disrupt it.

Sky Striker Favored

What does Sky Striker do?

It's a control deck built around one monster in the Main Monster Zone + powerful spell cards. They link summon Sky Striker Ace link monsters, use spells to generate advantage, and recycle everything. The more spells in their GY, the stronger their spells get (bonus effects at 3+ spells in GY). They grind you out with card advantage and disruption, not OTKs.

Key Cards to Know

Where to hand trap Sky Striker:
Ash Blossom → Engage! This is their best card. If Engage doesn't resolve, they lose their search + draw engine.
Impermanence → Kagari (stops Engage recycling) or Zero (stops the Raye/Roze summon).
Ghost Ogre → Widow Anchor (if they try to steal your monster).
Don't waste hand traps on Raye/Roze summon — they can just do it again.

Fiendsmith Even

What does Fiendsmith do?

Fiendsmith is a combo engine that tributes and equips monsters to build link/synchro/xyz boards. They use Fiendsmith's Requiem (tributes itself to summon Fiendsmith Engraver from deck), then equip Requiem from GY to Engraver. This leads into powerful generic boss monsters like D/D/D Wave High King Caesar and Necroquip Princess. They want to set up multiple negations.

Key Cards to Know

Where to hand trap Fiendsmith:
Ash Blossom → Requiem (when it tries to summon Engraver from Deck). Stops the entire combo.
Impermanence → Engraver (after it's summoned, negate before it equips Requiem).
Effect Veiler works here too — Fiendsmith monsters are on-field effects, not hand effects, so Veiler/Imperm are both live.

Mitsurugi Even

What does Mitsurugi do?

A Ritual-based combo deck. Their boss card End of the World Ruler can reveal itself from hand and pay 2000 LP to banish a Ritual Spell from deck and apply its effect — meaning they Ritual Summon by tributing monsters from the Deck (not hand!). This gives them easy access to powerful Ritual Monsters with built-in negation and protection. Very explosive but LP-intensive.

Key Cards to Know

Where to hand trap Mitsurugi:
Ash Blossom → End of the World Ruler's effect (when it banishes the Ritual Spell from Deck — this is a Deck search/activation).
Droll & Lock Bird is strong here if they search first.
Dimension Shifter hurts their GY recursion but doesn't stop the initial Ritual Summon.

K9 Vanquish Soul Even

What does K9 Vanquish Soul do?

A mid-range/control deck that reveals cards from hand to activate powerful effects. Vanquish Soul monsters can reveal FIRE/DARK/EARTH monsters from hand to bounce cards, burn LP, or banish. The K9 package adds Level 5 monsters that spam the board and bounce your cards as a Quick Effect. Their hand is essentially their resource — they don't commit everything to the board, making board wipes less effective.

Key Cards to Know

Where to hand trap K9 Vanquish Soul:
Ash Blossom → Razen (the search is their combo starter — stop it and they slow down).
Ash → Dr. Mad Love (searches their spells/traps).
Impermanence is weaker here — many VS effects activate by revealing from hand, so negating on-field effects doesn't stop everything.
Kaijus are great — they tend to have 1 key monster on board. Tribute it.

Maliss Slightly Unfavored

What does Maliss do?

An Alice in Wonderland–themed combo deck. They Normal Summon Maliss <P> White Rabbit, which sets a Maliss Continuous Trap from Deck. That trap banishes White Rabbit to summon Dormouse from Deck, then they keep chaining Maliss card effects to build a board of Link Monsters with multiple points of interaction. Their recursion is very strong and they can rebuild quickly after getting broken.

Key Cards to Know

Where to hand trap Maliss:
Ash Blossom → White Rabbit (when it tries to set the Continuous Trap from Deck). This is their key starter.
Impermanence → Dormouse (after it's summoned, before it can extend).
Nibiru can be strong here — they summon a lot before getting to their negation. Time it before they get 5+ summons.
This is your hardest matchup — their recursion matches yours. Win the die roll if you can.

Branded Dracotail (Mirror) Even

What is Dracotail?

It's another Branded variant that pairs the Branded engine with the Dragonmaid package. Dragonmaid monsters swap themselves during the Battle Phase for bigger dragons, giving extra bodies and triggers. The gameplan is the same as yours (Branded Fusion → Mirrorjade) but with Dragonmaid extenders that make the grind even longer.

Mirror match tips:
Mercourier is MVP — save its negate for their Mirrorjade's banish effect.
Whoever resolves Branded Fusion first usually wins — win the die roll and go first.
Ash their Branded Fusion, obviously. But if they have Fallen of White they can pivot.
Dimensional Barrier calling Fusion in the side deck is almost an auto-win in the mirror.

Radiant Typhoon Slightly Favored

What does Radiant Typhoon do?

A spell-heavy combo deck that generates advantage through chaining spell activations. They aim to OTK with big damage pushes. Less grindy than Branded — if you survive their push, you're usually favored in the long game.

Where to hand trap Radiant Typhoon:
Solemn Report (side deck) is incredible here — banishes their key spells.
Anti-Spell Fragrance slows them to a crawl.
Ash their first search spell to disrupt their chain-building.
